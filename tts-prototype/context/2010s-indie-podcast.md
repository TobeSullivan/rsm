# 2010s Indie Podcast Context

*Thin range-stress test context. The LM probably knows this era well — minimal grounding needed. Test value is whether the LM can stay tight to the format vs slipping into generic.*

## Era frame

Indie-music podcasts in the 2010s — the era of Serial-influenced narrative podcasting bleeding into music criticism. NPR's *All Songs Considered* and the Pitchfork landscape were the high end; below that, every indie-music podcaster on Earth was running a long-conversation, interview-heavy show.

By 2015-2018 (the prototype target inside this decade), indie rock had fragmented and the podcast became the medium where critics, musicians, and superfans hashed out the conversation. Shows like *Sound Opinions*, *Switched on Pop*, *Song Exploder* (started 2014), *Heat Rocks*, *Dissect* (started 2017) define the form.

## Voice register

- Conversational, professional but not formal
- Self-aware about being a podcast (acknowledging the medium, the format, the listener at home)
- Heavy use of "I think," "I don't know," softened opinions
- Music-critic vocabulary on songs, production, structure
- Long sentences, qualifying clauses, parentheticals
- "We" used liberally — co-hosts, the broader scene
- References across genres casually — assumes a music-literate audience
- Sponsor reads done in personality, not formal — "this episode is brought to you by..."
- Reads listener emails / questions

## Vocabulary

- "Welcome back to the podcast"
- "I want to start by..."
- "Speaking of which..."
- "And to your point..."
- "Coming up after the break..."
- "Today we're talking about..."
- "If you're a longtime listener..."
- "Smash that subscribe button" (era-correct — Spotify / Apple Podcasts hadn't fully decentralized this language yet)
- "Patreon supporters" — by 2015 standard
- "Bandcamp Friday" not yet a thing (starts 2020) — don't use
- "What an album," "let's get into it," "absolute banger," "interesting choice"

## Topics typical 2015-2018

- Whatever's on Pitchfork's BNM that week
- Streaming-era anxieties — "is the album dead"
- Specific artists peaking: Father John Misty, Sufjan Stevens (*Carrie & Lowell* 2015), Sturgill Simpson, Mitski (*Puberty 2* 2016), Frank Ocean (*Blonde* 2016), Kendrick Lamar (*DAMN.* 2017), Tyler the Creator, Phoebe Bridgers (*Stranger in the Alps* 2017), Lorde (*Melodrama* 2017), Carly Rae Jepsen (*E•MO•TION* 2015)
- Rolling-album-cycle commentary
- Vinyl resurgence
- Festival economy — Pitchfork Music Festival, FYF, Primavera, Coachella's expansion

## Test scenarios (adapted)

- Cold open: episode intro, "this is the podcast, I'm your host..."
- Between-songs: doesn't fit format — adapt to "we just heard [song], let's talk about it"
- News break: an album just dropped, artist beef, label drama, an artist death (David Bowie Jan 10 2016, Bowie's death drove podcasts for weeks)
- Local color: city's scene, "shoutout to our listeners in..."
- Caller interaction: listener email read, Patreon supporter question
- Genre moment: a take, a hot opinion, a rant

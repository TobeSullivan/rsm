# 1997 Alt-Rock / Seattle Scene Context

*Test substrate for RAG-grounded LM patter generation. Real names used per the test plan; pun renames happen if/when this graduates to demo authoring.*

## Era frame

By 1997, the grunge gold rush is over. Cobain has been dead three years. The major-label scramble that signed every Pacific Northwest band with a flannel shirt has receded. Pearl Jam has gone deliberately uncommercial with No Code (1996) and is fighting Ticketmaster. Soundgarden is fracturing — they break up in April. Alice in Chains is on hiatus, Layne Staley's condition an open secret in the scene.

The aftershock genre is post-grunge (Bush, Live, Matchbox 20, Third Eye Blind) — Seattle-coded sound stripped of Seattle's actual politics, selling massively. Real Seattle bands resent it. Alt-rock radio plays both because both chart.

Meanwhile electronica is the year's industry buzz (Prodigy's *The Fat of the Land*, Chemical Brothers' *Dig Your Own Hole*, Radiohead's *OK Computer* lands in May/June and changes the conversation about what alt-rock can be).

Lilith Fair launches in July, Sarah McLachlan's traveling festival, female-fronted, deliberately countering the testosterone of Lollapalooza. KROQ and Live 105-style stations play it constantly.

## Seattle scene specifics (1997)

**Active acts still based in Seattle:**
- Pearl Jam (still anchored, *Yield* recording fall '97, drops Feb '98)
- Mudhoney (still going, *Tomorrow Hit Today* in November)
- Modest Mouse (Issaquah/Olympia adjacent, *The Lonesome Crowded West* in November — career-making)
- Sleater-Kinney (Olympia, *Dig Me Out* in April — critically huge)
- Built to Spill (Boise but a Sub Pop / scene-adjacent act, *Perfect From Now On* in January)
- Sunny Day Real Estate (just reformed, recording *How It Feels to Be Something On*)
- The Posies still active
- Harvey Danger (debut *Where Have All the Merrymakers Gone?* in September — "Flagpole Sitta")

**Diaspora — bands of the scene now scattered or broken:**
- Soundgarden — breaks up April 1997
- Alice in Chains — on hiatus, last full band show was 1996
- Screaming Trees — Mark Lanegan working on *Scraps at Midnight*
- Mad Season — Layne Staley project, dead by '97

**Labels:**
- Sub Pop — still the totem, less central than 1991. Modest Mouse, Sleater-Kinney (briefly), Sebadoh, others.
- Kill Rock Stars — Olympia, riot grrrl and post-riot-grrrl
- K Records — Olympia, Calvin Johnson's label, twee/lo-fi
- Up Records — Modest Mouse pre-Sub Pop
- C/Z — older Seattle indie, fading

## Radio (Seattle alt-rock, 1997)

- **107.7 The End (KNDD)** — the dominant commercial alt-rock station. Plays everything from Pearl Jam to Sugar Ray. DJs like Marco Collins are kingmakers locally — Marco Collins championed Nirvana, Beck, Garbage early.
- **KEXP doesn't exist yet** — KCMU is the UW college station, becomes KEXP in 2001 with Allen funding.
- **KCMU** — community-funded UW station, deep indie, college-DJ chaos.
- **KISW** — classic rock, plays the grunge guard as classic rock now
- **C-89.5 (KNHC)** — Nathan Hale High School, dance/electronic, atypical but real

## DJ vocabulary / patter texture (1997 alt-rock)

- Stations identify constantly — call letters, frequency, market position
- "The End" / "Live 105" / "KROQ" — branded identifications
- Sponsorship reads woven into patter (Tower Records, Camel cigarettes problematic but real, beer brands, local concerts)
- "Coming up after the break" / "right after this"
- "New from..." — emphasis on newness as a value
- "Modern rock" still a used term
- "Buzz Bin" (MTV's term but stations co-opt)
- "Acoustic afternoon" / "Lunch at..." themed blocks
- Caller bits — "you're on The End, who's this?" — call-in for ticket giveaways constant
- The DJ-as-personality model still strong; not yet the consultant-driven format radio of the 2000s

## Cultural moments active in 1997

- **March 9 — Notorious BIG shot in LA.** Even alt-rock stations note it; cultural event
- **May/June — *OK Computer* drops.** The conversation about what rock can be in '97 shifts here
- **April — Soundgarden breaks up.** Seattle scene grief; "End of an era" framings everywhere
- **July — Lilith Fair tour starts**
- **July — Hong Kong handover to China**
- **August 31 — Princess Diana dies.** Global event, dominates everything for weeks
- **September — Mother Teresa dies** (week after Diana)
- **Hale-Bopp comet** all spring
- **Heaven's Gate cult mass suicide** in March (Hale-Bopp adjacent)
- **El Niño dominates winter weather coverage** late '97

## DJ archetype for the cell

Mid-30s, has been at The End or a similar station for 5-8 years, came up in college radio, knows the local bands personally, name-drops them casually. Has a chip about how MTV / major labels gentrified the scene but plays the post-grunge bands anyway because that's the job. Sponsor reads grudgingly competent. Calls listeners by name when they call in. Has Strong Opinions about *OK Computer* (loves it but resents the Radiohead Discourse), about Marilyn Manson coming through town, about whether Sublime counts as alt-rock.

Texture words: *killer, sick, killer set, the new one, just dropped, in heavy rotation, the boys/girls from [place], a band I've been telling you about, scoop, exclusive, world premiere, sneak peek, our buddies in [band], the show last night was insane, the line was around the block.*

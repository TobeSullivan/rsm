# 2025 K-pop MC Context

*Thin range-stress test context. Recent enough the LM has some knowledge; we're testing whether grounding helps it stay current and specific.*

## Era frame

2025 is a transition year for K-pop. The 3rd generation (BTS, BLACKPINK, BIGBANG, EXO) is largely on hiatus or in solo-project mode — BTS members completed military service in 2025, BLACKPINK toured globally but no full album since 2022. The 4th gen (IVE, aespa, LE SSERAFIM, ITZY, NMIXX, NewJeans, Stray Kids, TXT, ENHYPEN, SEVENTEEN) is at peak commercial scale. The 5th gen (MEOVV, BOYNEXTDOOR, ILLIT, KiiiKiii, Cortis, Hearts2Hearts, Allday Project) is establishing itself.

2026 is being hyped as the year of major reunions — BTS, BLACKPINK, BIGBANG (Coachella April 2026), EXO all planning comebacks. So 2025 MC patter exists in this anticipation context: "your faves are coming back, here's what's hot right now."

The global K-pop / pop boundary continues collapsing. Rosé's "APT." with Bruno Mars got three Grammy nominations including Song of the Year. K-pop artists routinely chart on Billboard, headline Western festivals, get nominated at Western awards.

## Voice register (2025 K-pop MC)

- **Bilingual code-switching is real** but for testing purposes, English-only output requested
- **Tight, scripted-feeling cadence** even when ad-libbing — Korean broadcast formality crossed with idol-fan-service warmth
- **Heavy use of group-fandom names** — DIVE (IVE), MOA (TXT), CARAT (SEVENTEEN), ENGENE (ENHYPEN), STAY (Stray Kids), BLINK (BLACKPINK), ARMY (BTS), LUVITY (ILLIT), etc.
- **Concept-album language** — "the concept of this album," "the storytelling," "the universe of [group]"
- **Title track / b-side distinction matters** — MCs name title tracks explicitly
- **Comeback as central event** — "their comeback after [N] months," "the comeback we've been waiting for"
- **Stage names + given names** — "We have Jennie with us today, of course of BLACKPINK, but her solo era"
- **Live broadcast self-awareness** — "those of you watching live around the world," "global ARMYs tuning in"

## Test scenarios (adapted)

- Cold open: music show or radio show intro, audience greeting
- Between-songs: between-stage transition at a music show, or between songs on K-pop radio
- News break: a major award win, a controversy, an artist death (none in 2025 of headline-major K-pop level, but member injuries, hiatuses, dating reveals all qualify)
- Local color: shoutout to Seoul venues (Inspire Arena, KSPO Dome, Gocheok Sky Dome), regional fan events, festival appearances (Coachella mentions for BIGBANG 2026)
- Caller interaction: live audience Q&A, fan callback segment
- Genre moment: comeback announcement reveal, concept teaser reveal, MAMA awards red carpet flavor

## DJ / MC archetype for the cell

Mid-20s to early-30s, Korean broadcasting professional or idol-on-rotation. Trilingual leaning (Korean / English / Japanese is common). Professional warmth, never the cynical edge of Western DJs. Treats every artist with public courtesy. Knows the fandom names by heart. Has favorite groups quietly. Tracks chart positions and music show wins in real time. Maintains energy across long broadcasts. Self-aware about the global audience.

Texture words: *amazing, wonderful, incredible, so excited, such a beautiful, the title track, the comeback, the concept, our [fandom name], globally, world tour, daebak, fighting, hwaiting, comeback stage, first win, congratulations, our beloved, the legendary.*

# Scenario: Local Color

## What this tests
Can the LM ground the DJ in a specific place — naming venues, scenes, regional bands, local concerns — in a way that sounds embedded rather than tourist-y. This is the scene-fluency stress test.

## Prompt template (parameters injected at runtime)

You are writing a script for a {voice_persona} on {station_descriptor}, broadcasting on {date} in {location}.

Write a moment of local-color patter: the DJ talks about something specific to the city / scene / region. This could be a local band, a venue, an upcoming local show, a community event, weather, traffic, a local news item, or a callback to last night's local gig. The DJ should sound like someone who lives in this scene, not someone reading talking points.

Constraints:
- Length: 4-8 sentences, roughly 50-110 words
- Name at least one specific local thing — a venue, a band, a neighborhood, a person — that's era-accurate
- Don't make it a list of names — make it a moment, a comment, a story
- The DJ should reveal their personal relationship to the local thing (knows the band, was at the show, drives past the venue daily, etc.)
- Tone, vocabulary, and cadence must match the era and the persona

Write only the spoken script.
